Apple Cinnamon Pork Chops

I once cooked these with a loved one; it's a great cooking experience and they're delicious too.

from gimmesomeoven.com, a much prettier website
  • 4 rib pork chops, bone in
  • Salt and pepper
  • 3 tbs butter, divided
  • 2 apples, thinly sliced and cored
  • 1 large white onion, halved and thinly sliced
  • 2 tbs packed brown sugar
  • 2 ts ground cinnamon
  • A pinch of ground cayenne pepper
  • 2/3 cup apple cider
  • 1/3 cup heavy cream
  1. Season pork chops with salt and pepper. Set them aside.
  2. Melt 2 tbs butter in large skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the chops and cook until brown.
    • About three minutes on each side.
  4. Transfer to a plate and let rest for 3 minutes.
  5. Melt remaining 1 tbs butter, adding the apples and onions to cook. Stir occasionally until onions are caramelized.
  6. Stir in brown sugar, cinnamon, and cayenne.
  7. Stir in cider and cream.
  8. Add the chops back in, and cook until internal temperature is 160 oF
    • If medium rare is desired, internal temperature should be 145 oF
  9. Serve chops with apple mixture on top.

    10. Glossary

    Caramelized
    The browning of sugar, often used in cooking. To tell if an onion is caramelized, see if it's translucent and soft.
    Medium rare
    A level of doneness used to describe cooked meat.
